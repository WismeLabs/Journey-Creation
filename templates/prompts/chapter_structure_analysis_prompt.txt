**STEP 1: Identify Chapter Type**
Read chapter title, introduction, and headings to classify as overview/deepdive/review/mixed

**STEP 2: Analyze Content Structure**

Count DISTINCT TOPICS:
- Not every heading = new topic
- Topic = Conceptually independent domain (e.g., "Microbes" vs "Electricity" = 2 topics)
- Sub-topics don't count (e.g., "Types of microbes" is sub-topic under "Microbes")

Identify RELATIONSHIPS:
- Interconnected = Concepts depend on each other, learning order matters
- Independent = Can learn in any order, no prerequisites
- Prerequisite sequence = A â†’ B â†’ C must be learned in order

**STEP 3: Determine Pedagogical Intent**

What is this chapter trying to achieve?
- **Breadth**: Show variety, generate interest, preview scope
- **Depth**: Master a topic completely, understand every detail
- **Connection**: Link multiple ideas, see relationships
- **Application**: Use knowledge in real scenarios, solve problems
- **Review**: Consolidate past learning, fill gaps
